Paying the bills

By trade I'm now an independent Information Technology Services and Support provider. It pays the bills, and affords me the time to earn an income while I develop stuff here at GitHub.

Before that I worked for a startup that created Mobile Command Posts for First Responder agencies, including some LA County area Police departments, Fire departments, and Sheriff agencies in that area. These MCPs were usually SUVs. Included in the rear cargo area was: three computers, a 30 inch writing tablet (for mapping and planning operations), three 20"-27" displays, police radios, satellite internet on a secured network, microwave video receiver, infrared video camera, and was all powered by a second car battery. And it powered on and off with a single button, and could be driven on unpaved roads while still functioning. Also built an EOC - Emergency Operations Center which was in operation years ago and used by the agencies mentioned.

One of the most impressive features of the MCPs and the EOC was the software suite developed in house by a team of really talented engineers. It was really what opened my eyes to the skill involved in creating of software applications. The app integrated Google Maps, Google Earth, tracked movements of work crews, tracked wind direction, used geolocation to identify other MCPs in the field, and received live video from helicopters, included tactical and operational planning features, including live notifications whenever an MCP came online. The application was known as the Antares X Suite.
